# Pokémon Emerald: Primal Harmony
## World State Document — v0.1
### Hoenn Geography: What Remains, What Changed, What Is Gone

---

## Overview

Primal Harmony's Hoenn is set approximately twelve to fourteen years after the events of the original Pokémon Emerald. The region is recognizable but altered — some changes dramatic and permanent, others slow and incremental. The world feels like it has been *working on something difficult* for a long time, and the results are uneven.

This document tracks the state of every significant location at the start of Phase Three (the player's young adult chapter), notes what has changed from the original game, and flags active instabilities relevant to gameplay.

## Southern Hoenn

### Littleroot Town ✓
Still a small residential community at the southern tip of the region. Population stable. The Birch family research presence has evolved — Professor Birch's lab now functions as the primary data clearinghouse for invasive species migration patterns across southern Hoenn. Birch himself is semi-retired; a younger research team runs daily operations.

**Notable change:** The lab's focus has shifted from trainer starter distribution to ecological survey coordination. Starter Pokémon are still available here, but they are not ordinary specimens — they are the corrupted starters, found in the field and brought to the lab for stabilization before the player claims them. The framing of the starter choice is entirely different from the original game.

### Oldale Town ~
Still a transit hub. The Pokémon Center has been upgraded with a Relocation Terminal. The town feels more functional and less pastoral than in the original — more people moving through on official business, fewer casual trainers passing between cities. There is a bulletin board near the Center that tracks current ecological alerts for the region, including active TOZ warnings.

### Petalburg City ~
Norman's gym is still here, but Norman is retired and the gym has new leadership. More importantly, the city's relationship to the surrounding Petalburg Woods has become complicated by the presence of **The Murk** (Poison-type TOZ in the woods). The city has installed air quality monitoring stations and temporary barriers around the western routes. Citizens are matter-of-fact about this — it is a problem they are managing, not a crisis they are panicking over.

**Petalburg Woods ⚠ — The Murk (Poison TOZ)**
The woods are heavily infested. Non-native Poison-types from Johto and Kanto have colonized the undergrowth. The native species have either adapted into Corrupted Variants or retreated to the zone's edges. Entry requires protective gear from the local Ranger station. This is an early mandatory Zone encounter.

### Rustboro City ~↑
The Devon Corporation has become significantly more involved in ecological infrastructure over the intervening years — they pivoted partially into environmental engineering, including levee construction consultation, Relocation Terminal manufacturing, and field equipment for Rehabilitators. The city is busier and feels more industrial than in the original, but the industry is now pointed at repair rather than extraction.

The Rustboro Gym still operates; its leader is an ecological steward as well as a rock specialist, with jurisdiction over the Route 116 area and the Rusturf Tunnel which has become an important species migration corridor.

**Granite Cave ↑ (Route 109 vicinity)**
The cave has been extensively mapped since the Dewford relocation — it extends further than originally charted, and the tunnel that led to New Dewford's valley is now a maintained passage with lighting and structural support. There is a small ranger station at the mouth.

## Dewford Archipelago

### Dewford Town ↕ (RELOCATED — see below)
**Old Dewford Coastal Site ✕↓**
The original Dewford Town coastal settlement is largely abandoned. Several structures remain partially standing but are considered unsafe. The docks still exist but are used only for memorial visits and the occasional Ranger station supply drop. The water around the old site is murky with sedimentation from the shifting seabed.

There is a memorial installation on the old main road — a collection of painted rocks contributed by former residents over the years. It is not officially maintained but it is clearly and continuously tended.

**New Dewford — Inland Valley ↑**
Accessible via the extended Granite Cave passage. The community relocated in stages over several years after successive storm seasons rendered the coastal site untenable. The Dewford cultural identity — surf culture, informal economy, close-knit social fabric — has been deliberately preserved and is a point of community pride.

New Dewford is built into and against the rock face of the valley. It is visually striking — layered construction, rope bridges between levels, a surprising amount of greenery cultivated in elevated garden beds. The Gym is here. The surf beach is gone but there is a freshwater lake in the valley where a new board-sport culture has developed around the different conditions.

**Player narrative note:** Visiting New Dewford for the first time should land as an emotional beat about adaptation. The memorial at the old site should be visitable at any point in the game. There is no quest there. It simply exists as a record.

### Granite Cave (Updated)
Still contains rare Pokémon and geological features. Now also contains the maintained passage to New Dewford, a Ranger station, and recent evidence of unusual rock formations in the deeper sections that Geological Survey teams have been monitoring — elevated mineral activity possibly connected to the Groudon awakening.

## Central Hoenn

### Mauville City ~↑ ⚠
Still the central hub of Hoenn but significantly expanded and altered. Mauville has leaned into its role as the region's logistics and communications center. The Pokémon Center is large and well-staffed. The Game Corner is gone — repurposed as a community coordination center.

**The Static Sprawl ⚠ — Electric TOZ (Mauville / New Mauville)**
This is the second TOZ the player encounters and the one that most directly disrupts city infrastructure. The underground New Mauville generator complex is heavily infested. The surface effects are real and inconvenient — intermittent electronics, sparking metal surfaces, skittish native Pokémon. The Pokémon Center's healing systems run at partial capacity until the zone is quelled.

The Mauville population has adapted with some irritability but also considerable ingenuity: rubber flooring in common areas, shielded relay equipment, a thriving secondary market in insulated gear.

### Route 117 — The Holdfast ↑
The Daycare has been expanded and renamed over the generations. The Holdfast now occupies a larger footprint on the route — additional pens, intake processing facilities, a small research annex, and staff quarters for volunteers who stay more than a few days. It is still recognizably a family operation, still warm and slightly cluttered, but it has grown into its role.

The route itself is one of the more ecologically stable areas of Hoenn — the proximity of a functioning rehabilitation waystation has created a kind of active stabilizing effect on the local encounter tables. Native Hoenn species are more common here than almost anywhere else in the central region.

### Verdanturf Town ~
Still a quiet residential town with good air quality. Has become something of a rest and recovery point for volunteer workers moving between Mauville and the western coast. The Contest Hall has been converted to a community gathering space.

### Fallarbor Town ~ ⚠
**Route 113 / The Ashen Glacier ⚠ — Ice TOZ**
This is the first TOZ the player encounters. The volcanic ash that gives Route 113 its distinctive grey character has been frozen solid — the result of concentrated Ice-type invasive pressure. The ash is suspended mid-fall in translucent ice sheets. The silence is wrong.

Fallarbor Town itself is partially isolated as a result. The flower shop — a key resource in the original game — is closed until the zone is quelled. The town has adapted with insulated structures and cold-weather Pokémon as working partners, but there is an air of waiting about the place.

Restoring this zone reopens the flower shop and restores a critical crafting node for restoration items. This is one of the game's earliest EHI-boosting opportunities.

### Meteor Falls ✓~
Structurally unchanged. The geological features that made it notable — the waterfall, the cave systems — are intact. There is now a small but active research station near the entrance, monitoring for seismic activity from the Mt. Chimney direction. The researchers are increasingly concerned.

## Mt. Chimney / Lavaridge Region

### Mt. Chimney / Lavaridge Town ⚠ ↓
**The Emberscar ⚠ — Fire TOZ (Mt. Chimney / Route 112 / Lavaridge)**
The Groudon awakening has directly destabilized this zone. Mt. Chimney's activity level is elevated far beyond baseline. The cable car to the summit is closed and has been for months — the infrastructure was damaged in a seismic event. Lavaridge Town's hot springs, while still functioning, are running hotter than normal and the surrounding area has been affected by Fire-type invasive overflow.

The Magma Hideout connection here is narratively significant: Team Obsidian maintains a field presence in the Mt. Chimney region, their operation in part because this is where Maxie once did his most consequential work. There are characters here who remember Team Magma and have complicated feelings about the people now working in similar caves for different reasons.

Restoring this zone reopens the cable car — a key traversal unlock — and is tied directly to the Groudon arc.

### Route 111 Desert ⚠
**The Crucible ⚠ — Ground TOZ**
The desert has expanded. Ground-type invasive pressure combined with Groudon's subsurface activity has extended the desert zone into areas that were previously grassland. The Mirage Tower appears and disappears with unusual frequency — possible connection to the subsurface instability.

Restoring this zone unlocks the Fossil Revival system, which has been mothballed due to the desert's expansion making safe excavation impossible.

## Eastern Hoenn

### Route 119 / Fortree City ⚠
**The Overgrowth ⚠ — Grass TOZ**
The Weather Institute is at the center of this. Grass-type invasive pressure combined with the disrupted weather system has created a zone of accelerated, uncontrolled growth — the native forest is being overtaken by invasive species, and the Weather Institute's instruments are affected by the biological interference. They are still broadcasting data, but it's unreliable in this zone.

Fortree's treehouse architecture has paradoxically made it more resilient — the structures flex rather than breaking. But the routes to and from the city are increasingly impassable without specific traversal tools.

Restoring this zone stabilizes the Weather Institute's output and, by extension, the global weather system reliability.

### Lilycove City ↑~
Lilycove has invested heavily in coastal defense infrastructure over the years. The harbor features significant levee and seawall construction, some of which was consulted on by Devon Corporation engineers. There is a visible Dutch polder-influenced design to portions of the water management system in the low-lying commercial district — reclaimed land, maintained drainage channels, functioning flood gates. The city works, and it works visibly.

The Department Store is still operational. The Contest Hall still runs. There is a solemnity to the city's relationship to the sea that wasn't there before — people look at the water differently here.

The Team Aqua hideout connection: Archie's old base is maintained as a Team Obsidian operational site, sealed from public access, flagged on Ranger maps as a restricted area.

### Mossdeep City ↓~
Mossdeep is worried. The island's elevation is not generous, and the Kyogre awakening has the residents monitoring sea levels with an attention that is no longer academic. The Space Center is still operational and is now contributing atmospheric and oceanic monitoring data to the broader ecological recovery effort, but the scientists there are operating with a kind of focused anxiety.

**The Veil ⚠ — Psychic TOZ (Mossdeep / Route 124)**
The Psychic-type invasive pressure in this zone has disrupted the Space Center's equipment in ways that are difficult to diagnose — the interference is mental as much as electronic. Pokémon in the area are behaving erratically. Human residents report mild persistent headaches and difficulty concentrating.

Restoring this zone stabilizes the Space Center and unlocks a late-story astronomical event.

## Southern Archipelago

### Pacifidlog Town ✕
**Gone.**

The floating platform community that existed for over a century on the open sea was destroyed in a seismic event approximately six years before Phase Three begins. The player saw the news report as a child. There is no rebuilding. There is no quest to restore it.

On the world map, Pacifidlog is marked with a ghost indicator — a greyed location icon beneath a wave symbol. Players with Dive can access the site. The wreckage is there. Some of the platforms are intact on the seafloor. There are no items of consequence. There is no music. The ambient sound is only the water.

Former Pacifidlog residents are scattered across Hoenn. They appear in Mauville, Slateport, Mossdeep — identified by a subtle visual detail (a color, a pin, a tattoo) that other characters in those cities know. There is no formal community structure but there is an informal network.

**Writers' note:** Do not give Pacifidlog a sidequest. Do not give it a recovery arc. It is the wound the game carries. It exists to be visited, not resolved.

### Sootopolis City ⚠ ↓
**The Abyss Bloom ⚠ — Water TOZ (Sootopolis / Cave of Origin / Route 126)**
The crater lake at the heart of Sootopolis is behaving strangely. The water level fluctuates. The color changes. The Cave of Origin — the site where Kyogre once surfaced — is sealed by Ranger order, its entrance guarded and monitored around the clock.

Sootopolis is not evacuated but it is tense. The community is divided between those who want to leave and those who are determined to stay and those who cannot afford to do either. The gym leader is still present but focused almost entirely on the containment situation rather than trainer challenges.

This is the penultimate major zone — resolving it is directly tied to the Kyogre arc and marks the approach to the game's final act.

## Sky and High Altitude

### Sky Pillar / Route 131 / Ever Grande ⚠
**The Static Sky ⚠ — Dragon TOZ**
The final zone. The Sky Pillar has always been remote and difficult to access. Now it is surrounded by Dragon-type invasive pressure of a scale that makes it genuinely dangerous to approach — not just difficult, but actively hostile to human and Pokémon alike.

Ever Grande City, sitting at the edge of this zone's influence, is experiencing unusual conditions: electrical storm activity, temperature inversions, visual distortions in the high atmosphere. Victory Road is passable but strange.

This zone is gated behind story progression and EHI thresholds. Attempting it underprepared is possible but will result in zone-wide resistance that makes progress nearly impossible.

Resolving this zone removes the Rayquaza Bond cap and triggers the game's endgame sequence.

## Route 117 — A Special Note

Route 117 — The Holdfast's home route — is one of the few locations in Hoenn that is consistently above-baseline in ecological health. This is not an accident. It is the result of over two decades of active rehabilitation work.

The route functions as the game's ecological anchor. Players returning to The Holdfast should feel the contrast between the route's relative stability and the zones they've been working in. The birdsong is fuller here. The grass is a more saturated green. Native Pokémon encounter rates are higher.

It is not paradise. It is what sustained care looks like over time.
