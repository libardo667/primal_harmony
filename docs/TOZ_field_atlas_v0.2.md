# Pokemon Emerald: Primal Harmony
## Type Overload Zones — Field Atlas
### Design Document v0.2 | 10 Zones | 30+ Corrupted Regional Variants

---

## Overview

This document maps all ten Type Overload Zones across Hoenn: geographic locations, environmental distortions, traversal hazards, native species caught in the surge, seeded Corrupted Regional Variants, quell strategies, and EHI restoration impact.

Zones are ordered by recommended narrative encounter sequence — roughly west-to-east with escalating stakes — though the open-world structure allows non-linear discovery. The final three zones (The Veil, The Abyss Bloom, The Static Sky) are gated behind story progression and EHI thresholds.

> **Content Note:** Each zone seeds 3 Corrupted Regional Variants as a starting roster. Additional variants for less prominent native species will be developed in the Corrupted Variant Design Bible (separate document).

# ZONE 01 — The Ashen Glacier
**Type:** Ice | **Location:** Route 113 / Fallarbor Town

> *Previously established in core design documents.*

## Overview
Route 113's volcanic ashfall has been frozen solid by a surge of Ice-type invaders from Sinnoh and Unova. What was once a soft, grey trudge through ash drifts is now a hazardous frozen slope — slick, unpredictable, and eerily beautiful.

## Environmental Distortion
The ash is frozen in mid-fall, suspended in translucent ice sheets. Visibility is poor due to frozen ash clouds. The ground is a frictionless slope requiring grip items or specific abilities to traverse without sliding.

## Traversal Hazards
Slip-and-slide physics on frozen surfaces. Certain paths only accessible with Cleats item or an Ice-type partner in the lead slot. Updrafts of frozen ash cause periodic whiteout conditions.

## Affected Native Species
- Spinda (Route 113)
- Skarmory (Route 113)
- Swablu (Route 114 adjacent)
- Zangoose / Seviper (Route 113)

## Seeded Corrupted Regional Variants

**Frostinda** (Corrupted Spinda) — *Ice / Normal*
> Its spots have become frozen patches of crystalline ice. Each spot is a different temperature, causing it to spin erratically on icy terrain. Dex: "Lost in a cold it didn't choose."

**Glaciory** (Corrupted Skarmory) — *Ice / Steel*
> Steel feathers now rimmed with permafrost. Can no longer fly — too heavy with ice buildup — and has adapted to skating across frozen surfaces instead.

**Ashviper** (Corrupted Seviper) — *Ice / Poison*
> Seviper's blade tail has become a shard of dark glacial ice. Venom is now a freezing compound. Dex: "Its rage has gone cold and still — more dangerous for it."

## Quell Strategy
Release Fire, Fighting, Rock, or Steel-type mons from any region via the Fallarbor Pokemon Center relocation terminal.

## EHI Restoration Impact
Restoring this zone thaws the ash back to its natural state and reopens the Fallarbor flower shop, which serves as a key crafting node for restoration items.

# ZONE 02 — The Static Sprawl
**Type:** Electric | **Location:** Mauville City / New Mauville

## Overview
New Mauville's underground generator complex has been overtaken by Electric-type invaders drawn to the power output. The surge has bled overground — Mauville City itself crackles with uncontrolled static, disrupting electronics, communications, and the nervous systems of any Pokemon or person who lingers.

## Environmental Distortion
The air smells of ozone permanently. Metallic surfaces spark and discharge at random. Wild Pokemon that aren't Electric-type are skittish and flee at double the normal rate. The Pokemon Center's healing systems are intermittent — heal counts as partial until the zone is quelled.

## Traversal Hazards
Certain metal-grate paths deliver periodic shocks unless a Ground-type leads the party. The underground New Mauville section is a maze of live wires and overloaded generators. Rubber Soles key item required for safe navigation of the core.

## Affected Native Species
- Voltorb (New Mauville)
- Electrike (Route 110)
- Magnemite (New Mauville)
- Minun / Plusle (Route 110)

## Seeded Corrupted Regional Variants

**Staticorb** (Corrupted Voltorb) — *Electric / Ghost*
> The surge has hollowed Voltorb out — it is now partially incorporeal, its interior a void of flickering light. It no longer Self-Destructs but instead releases a massive EMP pulse. Dex: "Something left when the current came in."

**Galvantrike** (Corrupted Electrike) — *Electric / Dark*
> Electrike that fed on unregulated power have become volatile and predatory. Their lightning bolts are black-tinged. They hunt in coordinated packs, overloading targets before striking.

**Fulgrite** (Corrupted Magnemite) — *Electric / Rock*
> Magnemite fused with the mineral deposits of New Mauville under electrical pressure, becoming partially crystallized. Can no longer float — skitters on the ground on rocky appendages.

## Quell Strategy
Release Ground-type mons via the Mauville City or Verdanturf Town relocation terminal. The underground New Mauville core requires a special Discharge Rod key item to manually reset before releases take effect.

## EHI Restoration Impact
Restoring the Static Sprawl reactivates the Mauville power grid properly, unlocking fast-travel between Pokemon Centers across Hoenn — a significant quality of life unlock tied to ecological progress.

# ZONE 03 — The Emberscar
**Type:** Fire | **Location:** Mt. Chimney / Lavaridge Town / Route 112

## Overview
Mt. Chimney was already volcanic. The arrival of Fire-type invaders from Johto and Unova has pushed it past equilibrium — the crater is erupting continuously at a low level, lava flows are encroaching on the cable car route, and Lavaridge Town is choked with sulphurous heat. Groudon's influence is detectable here, though whether it is causing the surge or being drawn to it is unclear.

## Environmental Distortion
Ambient temperature causes HP drain outside of battle for non-Fire, non-Rock, non-Ground types without a Heat Resist item. New lava flow obstacles block routes that were previously open. Ash fall from the crater reduces visibility on Route 112.

## Traversal Hazards
Lava flow obstacles require Water-type moves or specific HM equivalent to cool and cross. The cable car is non-functional. Certain areas only accessible at night when temperatures drop slightly.

## Affected Native Species
- Slugma (Route 112)
- Numel (Route 112)
- Torkoal (Route 111 adjacent)
- Machop (Mt. Chimney)

## Seeded Corrupted Regional Variants

**Pyrcoal** (Corrupted Torkoal) — *Fire / Ground*
> Torkoal's shell has cracked under the heat, filled in with hardened magma. It moves slower than ever but radiates intense ground-heating that scorches the earth beneath it. Dex: "It carries the mountain now. The mountain is not gentle."

**Scorchump** (Corrupted Numel) — *Fire / Steel*
> Numel's hump has vitrified — the heat has turned its fat reserves into a glassy, metal-like material. Runs hotter than ever and leaves scorched hoofprints on stone.

**Cinderchop** (Corrupted Machop) — *Fire / Fighting*
> Machop that refused to flee the heat have been tempered by it, their skin developing heat-resistant mineral deposits. Punches leave scorch marks. Deeply territorial around the crater.

## Quell Strategy
Release Water, Ground, or Rock-type mons via the Lavaridge Town relocation terminal. Quelling the Emberscar is also narratively tied to Groudon's arc — partial quell requires a story beat with Team Magma.

## EHI Restoration Impact
Restoring the Emberscar reopens the cable car route, makes Lavaridge's hot springs accessible (rest mechanic — full heal plus temporary stat boost), and triggers a Groudon Bond story beat.

# ZONE 04 — The Veil
**Type:** Psychic | **Location:** Mossdeep Island / Route 124

## Overview
Mossdeep was already a place of powerful psychic resonance. The arrival of Psychic-type invaders has amplified that resonance past coherence. The island is now partially displaced from normal perception — NPCs report seeing things that aren't there, Pokemon seem to respond to thoughts rather than actions, and the Space Center's instruments are reading impossible data.

## Environmental Distortion
Battle animations occasionally show 'ghost' moves — echoes of moves that weren't used. Wild encounters can be triggered by standing still. NPCs give contradictory directions. The island's geography feels subtly wrong — routes loop unexpectedly.

## Traversal Hazards
Requires a Clarity Lens key item to navigate accurately. Certain doors only open when approached from a specific direction. The Space Center's interior is a shifting maze at full Veil intensity.

## Affected Native Species
- Lunatone (Mossdeep area)
- Solrock (Mossdeep area)
- Wynaut (Mirage Island adjacent)
- Duskull (Route 121 adjacent)

## Seeded Corrupted Regional Variants

**Mirrortone** (Corrupted Lunatone) — *Psychic / Ghost*
> Lunatone now casts a visible duplicate of itself that acts one turn behind — a psychic echo. Its face has gone blank. Dex: "It no longer knows which of itself is real."

**Helicrack** (Corrupted Solrock) — *Psychic / Fire*
> Solrock that overcharged on ambient psychic energy now radiates intense heat alongside mental pressure. Its corona of light has become erratic and blinding. Cannot sleep.

**Spirallt** (Corrupted Wynaut) — *Psychic / Fairy*
> Wynaut, usually patient and observant, have become trapped in looping psychic feedback — endlessly mirroring signals that no longer have a source. Their expressions cycle rapidly and unpredictably.

## Quell Strategy
Release Dark-type mons via the Mossdeep City relocation terminal. Dark-types are immune to the psychic distortion and act as anchors. Quelling The Veil is also required to restore Space Center functionality.

## EHI Restoration Impact
Restoring The Veil stabilizes the Space Center, unlocking a late-game story event involving signals from outside Hoenn, and clarifies the Mirage Island location — a key optional content zone.

# ZONE 05 — The Dread Shore
**Type:** Dark | **Location:** Mt. Pyre / Route 122

## Overview
Mt. Pyre was always a place of mourning. The Dark-type surge has corrupted that solemnity into something predatory. The fog that normally drifts peacefully is now thick and disorienting. The native Ghost-types are behaving erratically — some have become aggressive in ways that contradict their nature entirely.

## Environmental Distortion
Permanent heavy fog reduces visibility to two tiles. Items on the ground are invisible until stepped on. Wild encounters are silent — no sound cue. Exp gain is halved on the mountain during the surge. The flame memorials at the summit are extinguished.

## Traversal Hazards
Requires a Spirit Candle key item to navigate the upper mountain. The item is obtained through a side quest recovering it from a Dark-type invader. Without it, the fog is disorienting and routes loop.

## Affected Native Species
- Shuppet (Mt. Pyre)
- Duskull (Mt. Pyre)
- Chimecho (Mt. Pyre summit)
- Vulpix (Mt. Pyre exterior)

## Seeded Corrupted Regional Variants

**Graveshuppet** (Corrupted Shuppet) — *Dark / Ghost*
> Shuppet's beak has grown sharp and its grudge-feeding has turned from passive absorption to active extraction. It seeks out sadness rather than waiting for it. Dex: "It stopped waiting. That was the change."

**Nightskull** (Corrupted Duskull) — *Dark / Ghost*
> Duskull's single eye has gone fully dark — it no longer glows. It navigates entirely by sensing fear. Its robe has become heavy and wet-looking, like something dredged from deep water.

**Ashfox** (Corrupted Vulpix) — *Dark / Fire*
> The memorial flames of Mt. Pyre were Vulpix's warmth source. The corruption of those flames has changed Vulpix's fire to a cold, dark-edged burn that chills rather than warms. Dex: "The fire it tends now offers no comfort — not even to itself."

## Quell Strategy
Release Fighting or Fairy-type mons via the Lilycove City relocation terminal. Quelling the Dread Shore also requires re-lighting the memorial flames — a story beat involving an elder NPC from Lilycove.

## EHI Restoration Impact
Restoring Mt. Pyre reactivates it as a story location, unlocks lore about the pre-crisis Hoenn ecology told through the memorials, and stabilizes the Ghost-type population — making them available as willing rehabilitator partners.

# ZONE 06 — The Murk
**Type:** Poison | **Location:** Petalburg Woods / Route 104

## Overview
Petalburg Woods is one of Hoenn's oldest and most biodiverse ecosystems. The Poison-type surge — likely arriving from Johto — has introduced a thick, bioluminescent miasma into the forest canopy. It looks almost beautiful at night. It is not. Native Bug and Grass-type species are sick and in some cases have been mutated by prolonged exposure.

## Environmental Distortion
Poisoned status applied to all party members without Antidote or Poison immunity every 30 steps. The bioluminescent haze makes the forest glow an eerie green-purple at night. Some trees have visibly wilted; others have mutated into spore-releasing hazard obstacles.

## Traversal Hazards
Spore obstacles block certain paths and must be cleared with specific moves or items. The deepest section requires a Gas Mask key item obtained from the Rustboro Devon Corporation as part of an early faction quest.

## Affected Native Species
- Shroomish (Petalburg Woods)
- Slakoth (Petalburg Woods)
- Wurmple (Route 104)
- Taillow (Route 104)

## Seeded Corrupted Regional Variants

**Venomish** (Corrupted Shroomish) — *Poison / Grass*
> Shroomish's spores have become highly toxic rather than sleep-inducing. Its cap has darkened and swollen. It no longer hops — it oozes. Dex: "The forest made it a warning. It didn't ask to be."

**Toxloth** (Corrupted Slakoth) — *Poison / Normal*
> Slakoth absorbed the miasma through prolonged sleep. It now secretes a mild paralytic through its fur. It still barely moves. The toxin is passive, not aggressive — Slakoth just stopped bothering to filter what it breathes out.

**Hazewing** (Corrupted Taillow) — *Poison / Flying*
> Taillow that fed on insects infected by the miasma have concentrated the toxins. Their distinctive red markings have turned a vivid warning purple. They leave a faint poisonous contrail in flight.

## Quell Strategy
Release Ground or Steel-type mons via the Petalburg City or Rustboro City relocation terminal. Quelling The Murk is an early-game milestone — it is the first zone the player is directed toward and serves as the tutorial for the relocation mechanic.

## EHI Restoration Impact
Restoring Petalburg Woods is the first major EHI milestone of the game. It reopens the woods as a safe travel route and triggers the first Aqua/Magma faction introduction scene.

# ZONE 07 — The Crucible
**Type:** Ground | **Location:** Route 111 Desert / Mirage Tower

## Overview
Route 111's desert was always inhospitable. The Ground-type surge has made it geological — the sands are now unstable, the Mirage Tower has been buried and re-emerged three times, and the desert's subsurface is cracking open in places, exposing hot vents and ancient rock formations. The fossils buried here are being disturbed.

## Environmental Distortion
Sandstorm is permanent and causes damage every step rather than every turn. Quicksand tiles have multiplied and are harder to spot. The desert's layout shifts daily — some paths that were open become buried, others newly exposed.

## Traversal Hazards
Requires Goggles key item for safe navigation. Without it, visibility is near zero. Certain buried paths require Dig move to access. The Mirage Tower's interior is a crumbling, shifting puzzle that changes each visit.

## Affected Native Species
- Trapinch (Route 111)
- Cacnea (Route 111)
- Baltoy (Route 111)
- Sandshrew (Route 111 extended)

## Seeded Corrupted Regional Variants

**Trembloch** (Corrupted Trapinch) — *Ground / Rock*
> Trapinch's pit-building instinct has been amplified — it now creates sinkholes large enough to swallow a trainer. Its jaws can crush stone. Dex: "It built a trap for the world. The world fell in."

**Fossilcnea** (Corrupted Cacnea) — *Ground / Grass*
> Cacnea that absorbed ancient mineral deposits from the cracked subsurface have grown a rocky exoskeleton over their spines. Slow but near-impervious to physical attacks.

**Ruintoy** (Corrupted Baltoy) — *Ground / Psychic*
> Baltoy has been resonating with the disturbed fossils and ancient strata. Its spinning has become erratic. It seems to be receiving signals from deep underground.

## Quell Strategy
Release Water, Grass, or Ice-type mons via the Mauville City or Lavaridge Town relocation terminal. Quelling the Crucible stabilizes the Mirage Tower permanently.

## EHI Restoration Impact
Restoring the Crucible unlocks the Fossil Revival system — ancient Hoenn fossils disturbed by the surge can now be recovered and revived, adding a set of prehistoric Hoenn-native Pokemon to the roster.

# ZONE 08 — The Overgrowth
**Type:** Grass | **Location:** Route 119 / Fortree City / Weather Institute

## Overview
Route 119 was already Hoenn's most relentlessly rainy, thickly vegetated route. The Grass-type surge has turned it into something primordial — the vegetation has grown explosive and semi-mobile, the Weather Institute is half-consumed by vines, and the aerial tramway into Fortree is completely overgrown. Fortree City's treehouse architecture is now genuinely threatened as the trees themselves have grown uncontrollably.

## Environmental Distortion
Movement speed halved by dense undergrowth unless Machete item equipped. Wild encounter rate is double. The Weather Institute's data is corrupted by the surge, making the overworld weather system unpredictable until this zone is quelled.

## Traversal Hazards
Certain paths require Cut or Machete to clear. Vine walls block previously open routes. The Weather Institute's interior is a multi-level tangle of vines and displaced equipment. Fortree's bridges are repositioned by tree growth.

## Affected Native Species
- Tropius (Route 119)
- Linoone (Route 119)
- Oddish (Route 119)
- Kecleon (Route 119 hidden)

## Seeded Corrupted Regional Variants

**Stormpius** (Corrupted Tropius) — *Grass / Dragon*
> Tropius that absorbed the surge's excess energy have grown dramatically — their leaf wings have expanded to tree-canopy scale. They are no longer gentle. Dex: "It remembers being small. It does not miss it."

**Rootbone** (Corrupted Linoone) — *Grass / Normal*
> Linoone that ran too long through the overgrowth have had plant matter take root in their fur. They leave a trail of sprouting growth behind them as they sprint. Cannot stop moving — the plant growth intensifies if they rest.

**Thorncleon** (Corrupted Kecleon) — *Grass / Normal*
> Kecleon have been colonized by the overgrowth — they now camouflage as vegetation rather than terrain, and their color-change stripe has been replaced with a growth ring pattern. Harder to find than ever.

## Quell Strategy
Release Fire, Bug, Ice, or Flying-type mons via the Fortree City or Mauville City relocation terminal. Quelling the Overgrowth also requires a story beat at the Weather Institute to restore its monitoring systems.

## EHI Restoration Impact
Restoring the Overgrowth restores the Weather Institute's function, stabilizing the overworld weather system globally. Fortree City's aerial layout is also restored.

# ZONE 09 — The Abyss Bloom
**Type:** Water | **Location:** Sootopolis City / Cave of Origin / Route 126

## Overview
Sootopolis sits inside a volcanic crater lake — already a place of extraordinary ecological fragility. Kyogre's prolonged presence has caused the crater to overflow in subtle, persistent ways. The Cave of Origin is emitting a continuous aquatic pressure. The city is slowly flooding. Not dramatically — just insistently. An inch per week. The residents are still there. They are worried.

## Environmental Distortion
Lower city areas are ankle-deep in water, affecting movement and obscuring items. The Cave of Origin is now accessible but its depths are flooded, requiring Dive. Certain native Water invaders from Johto have colonized the crater lake. Kyogre's influence is palpable — weather inside the crater is permanently heavy rain.

## Traversal Hazards
Flooded lower paths require Surf to cross. Cave of Origin's deeper levels require Dive. Certain routes through the crater walls are new — opened by water erosion. The Gym itself has flooded lower floors.

## Affected Native Species
- Seel (Route 126 adjacent)
- Luvdisc (Route 128 adjacent)
- Relicanth (Route 124/126 underwater)
- Corsola (Route 128)

## Seeded Corrupted Regional Variants

**Deepcorsola** (Corrupted Corsola) — *Water / Ghost*
> Corsola that have been submerged under Kyogre's overflow for too long have bleached completely — white-pale and hollow, their branch-coral now sharp and skeletal. They drift rather than swim. Dex: "The warmth left first. Then the color. Then it stopped noticing either."

**Abysslicanth** (Corrupted Relicanth) — *Water / Dark*
> Relicanth are ancient survivors. The abyss bloom has pulled them toward depth and darkness beyond their already deep range. They have developed bioluminescent lures and lost their rocky scale texture.

**Tidedisc** (Corrupted Luvdisc) — *Water / Psychic*
> Luvdisc caught in Kyogre's aura have absorbed trace psychic resonance. They now move in perfectly synchronized formations — dozens at a time, as if sharing a single mind.

## Quell Strategy
Release Electric or Grass-type mons via the Sootopolis City relocation terminal. The final quell requires a story beat co-authored with Team Aqua and Kyogre itself.

## EHI Restoration Impact
Restoring the Abyss Bloom is one of the two penultimate EHI milestones (alongside the Emberscar/Groudon arc). Completing both triggers the final alliance sequence with Kyogre, Groudon, and Rayquaza.

# ZONE 10 — The Static Sky
**Type:** Dragon | **Location:** Sky Pillar / Route 131 / Ever Grande approaches

## Overview
Sky Pillar reaches into the upper atmosphere — it is Rayquaza's domain, the axis between sea and sky. Dragon-type invaders from Unova and Kalos, drawn to the concentrated draconic energy, have colonized the Pillar's upper reaches and are generating a resonance interference that is disrupting Rayquaza's signal. **The Rayquaza Bond Meter is capped until this zone is addressed.**

## Environmental Distortion
The Pillar's crumbling floors are additionally hazarded by draconic energy discharge — random tiles deal Dragon-type damage. The upper floors are wreathed in aurora-like light that obscures the ceiling. The routes approaching Sky Pillar (Route 131) experience persistent high wind that blows the player backward on exposed cliff sections.

## Traversal Hazards
Requires Mach Bike or a Flying-type lead to navigate the wind sections of Route 131. The Pillar's interior requires navigating energy discharge tile patterns. The very top requires a sufficient Rayquaza Bond level — the door is sealed by draconic resonance that only opens to a recognized presence.

## Affected Native Species
- Swablu (Route 131)
- Altaria (Route 131)
- Flygon (evolved from Route 111 Trapinch — a returning Hoenn native)

## Seeded Corrupted Regional Variants

**Voidblu** (Corrupted Swablu) — *Dragon / Flying*
> Swablu's cloud-cotton wings have become translucent and charged with draconic energy. It floats higher than ever — so high it rarely comes down. Its song, usually soothing, now carries a harmonic that causes unease. Dex: "It sings from so far up that no one can tell it is still singing."

**Stormtaria** (Corrupted Altaria) — *Dragon / Flying*
> Altaria that absorbed the Static Sky's resonance have grown more angular — their cloud wings have developed visible internal lightning. They are no longer gentle. They circle the Pillar ceaselessly.

**Abyssgon** (Corrupted Flygon) — *Dragon / Ground*
> Flygon that made their way to the Pillar have been changed by altitude and draconic saturation. Their eye-covers have grown opaque and dark-tinted, their frequency hum now registers as a low draconic resonance that interferes with other signals.

## Quell Strategy
Release Ice or Fairy-type mons via the Pacifidlog Town or Ever Grande City relocation terminal. Quelling the Static Sky requires maximum Rayquaza Bond AND completion of both the Emberscar and Abyss Bloom arcs — it is the final ecological zone, and clearing it triggers the endgame sequence.

## EHI Restoration Impact
Restoring the Static Sky removes the Rayquaza Bond cap, triggers the final cooperative encounter sequence, and allows access to Sky Pillar's summit — the location of the game's climactic story resolution.

# Corrupted Starter Evolution Paths

The three Hoenn starters are found in deeply corrupted states with their original typings entirely displaced. Each has three divergent evolution paths depending on rehabilitation approach: full corruption (no healing), partial healing (one cleanse), and full restoration (both cleanses). Each path branches at level 16 and level 36 — producing six possible final forms per starter, eighteen total.

> **Type Note:** All starter corruption types are intentionally distinct from the ten TOZ types. Starter corruptions use: Bug, Rock, Fighting, Steel, Ghost, and Fairy.

---

## TREECKO — Corrupted Typing: Bug / Rock

*Found buried beneath a rockslide colonized by Bug-type invaders from Johto. Sustained contact replaced its chlorophyll with chitin and minerals. Its corruption is one of substitution: something else grew in where the green life used to be.*

### Path A — Fully Corrupted (Bug / Rock) | No Healing

**Scleecko** — Bug / Rock — Level 16
> Treecko's skin has hardened into chitinous plates, its green entirely faded to grey-brown. The adhesive pads on its feet have become rock-boring hooks. Stat focus: Defense, Speed. Dex: "It forgot it could photosynthesize. The stone remembers for it now."

**Carapecko** — Bug / Rock — Level 36
> A fully armored gecko with a layered exoskeleton carapace, mandibles, and rocky dorsal spines. The tail-tip has become a scorpion-like mineral spike. Stat focus: Attack, Defense, Speed. Dex: "The forest it came from would not recognize it. That does not trouble Carapecko. Carapecko does not think of forests."

### Path B — Partial Healing (Grass / Bug) | One Cleanse

**Mossecko** — Grass / Bug — Level 16 with one cleanse
> The green is returning but the chitinous elements remain — leafy wing-cases have grown over the rocky plates, and the tail now produces silk. It photosynthesizes through translucent carapace panels. Stat focus: Sp. Atk, Speed. Dex: "Half-remembered. The leaf and the shell are negotiating."

**Silkvine** — Grass / Bug — Level 36 with one cleanse
> A sleek, vine-covered gecko with silk-strand webbing between its limbs used for gliding. Stat focus: Sp. Atk, Speed, Sp. Def. Dex: "Half-remembered. Half-becoming. It has decided both halves are worth keeping."

### Path C — Full Restoration (Grass) | Both Cleanses

**Leafecko** — Grass — Level 16 with both cleanses
> The chitin has shed completely, replaced by healthy vibrant scales and broad leaf-pads on the feet. Stat focus: Speed, Sp. Atk. Dex: "It returned to itself. The shape of the return is not quite the shape it left."

**Verdacko** — Grass — Level 36 with both cleanses
> Fully restored — a powerful acrobatic gecko wearing a dense, living leaf mantle that shifts with its mood. The fastest of its three final forms. Stat focus: Speed, Sp. Atk, HP. Dex: "To be healed twice is to be trusted twice. Verdacko does not forget a debt of patience."

## TORCHIC — Corrupted Typing: Fighting / Steel

*Found in an industrial corridor near Rustboro where Steel-type invaders and Fighting-type migrants created a sustained pressure zone. The warmth was not destroyed — it was refined out. What remained was harder, colder, and very strong.*

### Path A — Fully Corrupted (Fighting / Steel) | No Healing

**Clenchic** — Fighting / Steel — Level 16
> Torchic's fluffy down has been replaced by interlocking metallic feather-plates. Its feet are now weighted iron talons. Cannot learn Fire-type moves. Stat focus: Attack, Defense. Dex: "The warmth was refined out of it. What the process left behind does not miss the warmth."

**Forgechic** — Fighting / Steel — Level 36
> A heavy, powerful iron-plated raptor bearing almost no resemblance to its soft origin. Relentless and efficient in combat, with layered armor and talons that can shear stone. Slowest of its three final forms. Stat focus: Attack, Defense, HP. Dex: "It became what the pressure demanded. The question of whether it wanted to no longer applies."

### Path B — Partial Healing (Fire / Fighting) | One Cleanse

**Embrawl** — Fire / Fighting — Level 16 with one cleanse
> The metallic plates are visibly cracking — ember-orange light bleeds through the fractures. Torchic is starting to remember heat. Stat focus: Attack, Sp. Atk. Dex: "The fire is remembering its name. The steel is not ready to let go."

**Scorcombat** — Fire / Fighting — Level 36 with one cleanse
> A blazing warrior-bird where fire and steel coexist — ember-bright plating, burning talons, fierce and beautiful. Stat focus: Attack, Sp. Atk, Speed. Dex: "Two natures, neither surrendered. It decided the tension was a weapon."

### Path C — Full Restoration (Fire) | Both Cleanses

**Kindlic** — Fire — Level 16 with both cleanses
> The steel has fully shed, fallen away in flakes that glow briefly before going cold. Fluffy, warm, ember-bright — recognizably Torchic again. Stat focus: Sp. Atk, Speed. Dex: "It remembers the cold. That is why the warmth means more now."

**Blazeborn** — Fire — Level 36 with both cleanses
> Fully restored to its evolutionary apex. The fastest of the three final forms. Its restored form is more vivid than standard, as if the healing over-corrected slightly toward brightness. Stat focus: Sp. Atk, Speed, Attack. Dex: "It burned through what it was not, to find what it was. Nothing about it is diminished by the journey."

# Open Design Questions — Zones & World

- **Zone sequencing:** Is the recommended order enforceable or purely suggested? Are any zones truly locked vs. just narratively implied?
- **Zone discovery:** How does the player learn a new zone has appeared? Environmental cues, NPC distress calls, Pokedex alerts?
- **Zone expansion:** Zones pulse and expand if left unaddressed — what is the mechanical clock on this? Is it real-time, step-based, or story-beat-triggered?
- **Partial quell states:** Can a zone be at 25%, 50%, 75% restored with incremental effects, or is it binary (infested / restored)?
- **Zone overlap:** Some areas are adjacent — can two zones merge or bleed into each other? What happens at the border of The Murk and The Ashen Glacier?
- **Corrupted Variant roster expansion:** The 30 seeded variants here are starting points. Full variant design bible needed for all native Hoenn species across all zones.
- **Legendary adjacency:** Zones 03, 09, and 10 are tied to Groudon, Kyogre, and Rayquaza respectively. Are the legendaries causing the surges, drawn to them, or trapped by them?

# Open Design Questions — Starter Lines

- **Stat spreads:** Each of the 18 forms needs a full BST and stat distribution. Suggested approach: corrupted paths skew toward Attack/Defense, restoration paths skew toward Speed/Sp. Atk, hybrid paths are balanced.
- **Learnsets:** Corrupted forms should have access to their corruption-type moves; restored forms recover the original line's learnset. Hybrid path gets a blend. Full learnset document needed.
- **Held item interaction:** Does the Everstone behave differently on a corrupted Pokemon? Can it lock a form mid-cleanse?
- **Naming convention:** The six forms per starter are named here as design placeholders. Final names need a pass for consistency and feel.
- **Corruption source locations:** Each starter's corruption has a suggested origin. Are these findable locations in the world? Does visiting them provide lore?
